## How Purity Affects Sound

Different purities produce different acoustic characteristics:

| Purity | Characteristics | Example Coins |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **.9999 (24k)** | Softer metal, shorter ring | Maple Leaf, Buffalo |
| **.999 (fine)** | Standard bullion sound | Silver Eagles, Britannias |
| **.9167 (22k)** | Harder alloy, longer ring | Krugerrand, American Eagle |
| **.900** | Durable, bright ring | Morgan/Peace Dollars |

The Canadian Silver Maple Leaf's .9999 purity gives it a notably shorter ring compared to .999 coins — this is normal, not a sign of counterfeiting. See [False Positives](/false-positives) for details.

## Requesting New Coins

Want a coin added? Here's how to help:

### Option 1: Submit Through the App
If you own an authentic coin we don't support yet:
1. Use the app's "Submit Coin" feature
2. Record a clean tap of your coin
3. Provide coin details (weight, dimensions, composition)
4. We'll analyze and add it to the database

### Option 2: Community Request
- [Open a GitHub issue](https://github.com/jessems/pingcoin-android/issues)
- [Join our Discord](https://discord.gg/pingcoin)
- Provide as much detail as possible:
  - Official mint name and specifications
  - Links to official product pages
  - Why this coin should be prioritized (popularity, counterfeit risk)

### Prioritization
We add coins based on:
1. **Popularity** — More users testing = higher priority
2. **Counterfeit risk** — Coins with known fake issues
3. **Data availability** — We need authentic reference recordings

## Coin Families

Some coins come in multiple weights but share the same design family:

**American Eagle Family:**
- Gold: 1 oz, 1/2 oz, 1/4 oz, 1/10 oz
- Silver: 1 oz, 5 oz

**Canadian Maple Leaf Family:**
- Gold: 1 oz, 1/2 oz, 1/4 oz, 1/10 oz
- Silver: 1 oz, 1.5 oz

Each weight requires separate calibration because frequency depends on dimensions, not just material.

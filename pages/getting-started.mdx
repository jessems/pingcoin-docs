Get up and running with Pingcoin in under five minutes.

## Your First Test

### Step 1: Choose Your Coin Type

Open the app and select the coin you want to test from the list. Make sure you choose the correct:
- Metal type (gold or silver)
- Denomination (1 oz, 1/2 oz, etc.)
- Specific coin (American Eagle, Maple Leaf, etc.)

Getting this wrong will produce a failed result even for authentic coins.

### Step 2: Prepare Your Environment

For best results:
- Find a **quiet room** — background noise interferes with analysis
- Place the coin on a **soft surface** (felt, cloth) — hard surfaces create echoes
- Or balance it on your **fingertip** — this allows free vibration

### Step 3: Tap the Coin

When the app is ready to record:

1. **Tap the coin** with another coin or a small metal object
2. **Strike near the edge** — produces the clearest ring
3. **Let it ring** — don't stop the vibration prematurely
4. **One clean tap** — avoid multiple taps during recording

### Step 4: Review the Results

The app will display:
- **Verdict** — Pass, Fail, or Inconclusive
- **Frequency spectrum** — Visual graph of detected frequencies
- **Match confidence** — How closely the frequencies align with reference data

## Tips for Accurate Results

### Do ✅

- Test in a quiet environment
- Use a consistent tapping technique
- Let the coin ring freely
- Try multiple tests if results are inconclusive
- Clean your coin first (dirt dampens vibration)

### Don't ❌

- Test near loud appliances or music
- Hold the coin tightly (dampens vibration)
- Tap multiple times during recording
- Use a plastic or wooden striker
- Rush the process

## What If the Test Fails?

A failed test doesn't necessarily mean your coin is fake. Check these common causes:

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Selected wrong coin type | Verify the exact coin denomination |
| Background noise | Move to a quieter location |
| Poor tap technique | Practice getting a clean ring |
| Coin is dirty | Clean and dry the coin |
| Microphone issues | Test with a coin you know is authentic |

If you consistently get failed results on a coin you believe is genuine, see our [Troubleshooting](/troubleshooting) guide.

## Building Your Testing Routine

For regular coin buyers, we recommend:

1. **Test every new purchase** — Better to catch fakes early
2. **Document results** — The app can store test history
3. **Use multiple methods** — Combine acoustic testing with weight checks and visual inspection
4. **Trust your instincts** — If something feels wrong, investigate further
